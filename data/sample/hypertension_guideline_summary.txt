Title: Hypertension Management — Educational Summary
Note: Original synthetic summary written for demo purposes; not a clinical guideline.

1. Definitions (adult, office BP)
- Normal: < 120/80 mmHg.
- Elevated: 120–129 systolic AND < 80 diastolic.
- Stage 1 hypertension: 130–139 / 80–89 (per ACC/AHA 2017) or ≥ 140/90 in
  some other guidelines.
- Stage 2 hypertension: ≥ 140 / ≥ 90.
- Hypertensive urgency: ≥ 180/120 without acute end-organ damage.
- Hypertensive emergency: ≥ 180/120 with acute end-organ damage.

2. Diagnosis
Hypertension should not be diagnosed on a single measurement. Confirm with
out-of-office measurements where possible (home BP monitoring or 24-hour
ambulatory BP monitoring) to exclude white-coat hypertension and identify
masked hypertension.

3. Lifestyle modification (first-line for everyone)
- DASH-style dietary pattern: rich in vegetables, fruits, whole grains, low-fat
  dairy; reduced saturated fat and sodium.
- Sodium restriction (commonly < 2.3 g/day, ideally < 1.5 g/day).
- Weight loss in overweight/obese individuals; ~1 mmHg drop per kg lost on
  average.
- Aerobic and dynamic resistance exercise (≥ 150 min/week moderate-intensity).
- Limit alcohol to ≤ 2 drinks/day (men) and ≤ 1 drink/day (women).
- Smoking cessation (for cardiovascular risk reduction overall).

4. Pharmacologic therapy — first-line classes
- Thiazide-type diuretics (e.g., chlorthalidone, indapamide preferred over
  hydrochlorothiazide for outcomes data and longer half-life).
- ACE inhibitors (e.g., lisinopril) or ARBs (e.g., losartan).
- Calcium channel blockers, dihydropyridine type (e.g., amlodipine).
Initial monotherapy is reasonable in stage 1 with low cardiovascular risk;
combination therapy is often preferred when BP is ≥ 20/10 mmHg above goal
or in stage 2.

5. Compelling indications (favored agents)
- Diabetes with albuminuria: ACEi or ARB.
- Heart failure with reduced ejection fraction: ARNI / ACEi / ARB +
  beta-blocker + MRA + SGLT2 inhibitor.
- Post-myocardial infarction: beta-blocker + ACEi or ARB.
- Black adults without compelling indication: thiazide or CCB often preferred
  initially; combine with ACEi/ARB if needed.
- Pregnancy: labetalol, nifedipine ER, or methyldopa. Avoid ACEi, ARBs,
  direct renin inhibitors, and aldosterone antagonists.

6. Treatment targets
For most adults, a target office BP < 130/80 mmHg is reasonable based on
SPRINT-era evidence, with attention to orthostatic symptoms and frailty.
In older adults or those with multiple comorbidities, individualize the
target to balance benefit and the risk of falls and acute kidney injury.

7. Resistant hypertension
Defined as BP above goal despite adherence to ≥ 3 antihypertensives at
optimal doses, ideally including a diuretic. Consider:
- Confirming adherence and accurate measurement.
- Screening for secondary causes (primary aldosteronism, OSA, renal artery
  stenosis, pheochromocytoma, Cushing, drug-induced).
- Adding a mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (e.g., spironolactone) as
  fourth-line therapy (PATHWAY-2).
