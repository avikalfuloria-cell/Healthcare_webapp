Title: SGLT2 Inhibitors in Heart Failure — Educational Summary
Note: Original synthetic summary written for demo purposes.

1. Class overview
Sodium-glucose cotransporter-2 (SGLT2) inhibitors block glucose reabsorption
in the proximal renal tubule, producing glucosuria and modest natriuresis.
The class includes empagliflozin, dapagliflozin, canagliflozin, and
ertugliflozin. Although developed for type 2 diabetes, several agents have
demonstrated benefit in heart failure independent of diabetes status.

2. Heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF)
Two large randomized trials (DAPA-HF with dapagliflozin and EMPEROR-Reduced
with empagliflozin) showed a statistically significant reduction in the
composite of cardiovascular death or worsening heart failure in patients
with HFrEF (LVEF ≤ 40%), regardless of baseline diabetes status. Number
needed to treat (NNT) was approximately 19–21 over roughly 1.5 years.

3. Heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (HFpEF)
EMPEROR-Preserved (empagliflozin) and DELIVER (dapagliflozin) extended these
benefits to patients with LVEF > 40%. The relative risk reduction in heart
failure hospitalizations was meaningful, while the impact on cardiovascular
mortality was smaller and less consistent than in HFrEF.

4. Renal effects
SGLT2 inhibitors reduce progression of chronic kidney disease (CKD) and the
risk of end-stage kidney disease in patients with diabetic and non-diabetic
CKD, as shown in trials such as DAPA-CKD and EMPA-KIDNEY. There is typically
a small initial dip in eGFR after initiation that is reversible and does not
warrant discontinuation.

5. Adverse effects
- Genital mycotic infections (more common in women).
- Volume depletion, especially when combined with diuretics.
- Diabetic ketoacidosis, including euglycemic DKA — rare but important to
  recognize, particularly perioperatively.
- Lower-limb amputation signal historically associated with canagliflozin in
  CANVAS; subsequent data have been more reassuring.
- Fournier gangrene — very rare necrotizing fasciitis of the perineum.

6. Practical considerations
- Hold the drug during acute illness with poor oral intake or planned major
  surgery to reduce DKA risk.
- Consider down-titrating loop diuretics on initiation to mitigate volume
  depletion.
- Initiation is generally safe down to eGFR ≈ 20 mL/min/1.73 m², depending
  on the agent and the indication.

7. Guideline status
Major heart failure guidelines (e.g., ACC/AHA/HFSA, ESC) now recommend an
SGLT2 inhibitor as part of foundational guideline-directed medical therapy
for HFrEF, alongside an ARNI (or ACEi/ARB), beta-blocker, and mineralocorticoid
receptor antagonist. Recommendations for HFpEF are increasingly supportive
based on EMPEROR-Preserved and DELIVER.
